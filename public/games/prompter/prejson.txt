<p>Prompts are starting points to be used in games. They are just used to give your mind direction. They are the equivalent of when an improv troupe asks for suggestions from the audience.  If a prompt fails to spark anything, try to think about it more abstractly. How does it make you feel? Break it down into smaller components, explore the concept.</p>

<p>OMusing features an in-browser prompter that can be used to get you started. But, there’s all sorts of sources for prompts:</p>

<ul>
	<li style='list-style-type:circle'>Ask a friend</li>
	<li style='list-style-type:circle'>Use your passions</li>
	<li style='list-style-type:circle'>Find a random post in your social media stream</li>
	<li style='list-style-type:circle'>Open a book, magazine, or newspaper to a random page</li>
	<li style='list-style-type:circle'>Trending <a href='http://twitter.com/'>Twitter</a> tags</li>
	<li style='list-style-type:circle'>Check out <a href='https://www.reddit.com/r/WritingPrompts/'>Reddit writing prompts</a></li>
	<li style='list-style-type:circle'>Billboards while travelling</li>
	<li style='list-style-type:circle'>Just look around. Anything could lead you somewhere.</li>
</ul>
